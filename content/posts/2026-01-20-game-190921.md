Japan's console game market experienced a staggering growth of 138.8% in 2025, primarily fueled by the launch of the Nintendo Switch 2. According to a report from Kadokawa Game Linkage and Famitsu, the market reached an impressive 418.13 billion yen (approximately $2.64 billion USD) by the end of the year, showcasing a remarkable surge in both hardware and software sales.

The Nintendo Switch 2 was a game-changer, selling 3.784 million units in Japan alone, while the original Switch added another 1.52 million units to its tally. This growth was complemented by strong sales of popular titles such as Mario Kart World and Pokmon Legends: Z-A, which dominated the charts. With anticipated releases like Dragon Quest VII Reimagined and Resident Evil Requiem on the horizon, the future looks bright for the Japanese gaming landscape.
